# How Much Does LASIK Eye Surgery Cost in India? (Mumbai Compared)

If you are trying to figure out how much LASIK eye surgery costs in India, the honest answer is: it depends heavily on the procedure type, the technology, the city, and the clinic. In 2026, you will find prices ranging from ₹20,000 per eye for standard LASIK at a budget centre to over ₹1,10,000 per eye for the newest flapless SILK platform at a premium Mumbai clinic. This guide breaks down every cost variable, compares major Indian cities side by side, and explains exactly what you get — and what you give up — at each price point.

---

## Quick Answer: National LASIK Price Range in India (2026)

For both eyes combined, LASIK in India currently costs:

- **Budget end (Standard LASIK):** ₹40,000–₹80,000 for both eyes
- **Mid-range (Femto/Bladeless or Contoura):** ₹1,10,000–₹2,00,000 for both eyes
- **Premium (SMILE or SILK):** ₹1,60,000–₹2,20,000 for both eyes

These are all-in estimates at reputable clinics. Advertised prices that look significantly lower almost always exclude pre-op tests, medications, or follow-up visits.

## What Drives Price Differences Between Cities

Several structural factors create the cost variation you see across Indian metros:

### 1. Real Estate and Operating Costs
Mumbai and Delhi have significantly higher clinic overhead than Pune or Hyderabad. A portion of every procedure price covers rent, staff, and utilities in high-cost urban centres.

### 2. Equipment Vintage and Depreciation
A clinic that bought a femtosecond laser in 2018 and has fully depreciated it can charge less than one that installed the latest VisuMax 800 in 2025. Newer platforms deliver better outcomes but carry higher cost recovery requirements.

### 3. Surgeon Seniority and Reputation
A surgeon with 20,000+ procedures and a published research profile commands a premium. In Mumbai, the density of fellowship-trained refractive surgeons is among the highest in India, which means both higher floor and ceiling pricing.

### 4. Volume Economics
High-volume clinics in Hyderabad and Chennai can sometimes offer lower per-procedure costs because fixed costs are spread across more patients per day.

### 5. Procedure Mix
Cities with higher per capita income tend to see more demand for premium procedures (SMILE, SILK, Contoura), which pushes average prices up even for standard procedures at the same clinics.

## Is Mumbai Expensive for LASIK? (The Honest Answer)

On a per-procedure basis, Mumbai prices sit slightly above the national median but are not the most expensive city in India — Delhi's premium clinics match or exceed Mumbai pricing for equivalent technology. More importantly, Mumbai offers something the price comparison alone does not capture:

- The **highest concentration of fellowship-trained refractive surgeons** of any Indian city
- **Multiple competing premium clinics** (including Surya Eye, Ojas Eye Hospital, Shroff Eye Centre, and others) that drive quality standards upward across the board
- **Post-operative care infrastructure** — if something goes wrong after LASIK, you want access to multiple expert second opinions nearby

For patients considering travelling from Tier 2 or Tier 3 cities, the calculus often favours Mumbai: the marginal cost of travel and accommodation is typically offset by access to better screening protocols and more experienced surgeons. For a ₹80,000–₹1,00,000 investment in permanent vision correction, the risk of under-equipped screening at a lower-cost centre is not worth the saving.

## What the "₹15,000 LASIK" Ads Really Mean

You will encounter advertisements in Mumbai and other cities promoting LASIK at ₹15,000–₹18,000 per eye. Here is what that price point actually involves:

- **Procedure type:** Almost always standard LASIK with a microkeratome blade — the oldest and least precise flap creation method
- **Patient eligibility:** Typically available only for low-to-moderate myopia (−1.00 to −4.00D) with normal corneas — the easiest cases
- **Inclusions:** The advertised price usually excludes the pre-op workup (₹1,500–₹3,500), post-op medications (₹1,500–₹2,500), and follow-up visits
- **Technology:** Older laser platforms with lower pulse repetition rates
- **Surgeon involvement:** At this price point, query carefully about who will actually be performing your procedure

This is not to say that standard LASIK at ₹20,000 per eye is inherently dangerous — it can be appropriate for suitable candidates at well-run clinics. The problem is when the low advertised price creates a bait-and-switch situation, or when the real cost of the complete treatment episode is disguised.

**Always ask for a written all-inclusive quote** before comparing prices between clinics.

## FAQ

### How much does LASIK cost in India?

In 2026, LASIK costs between ₹18,000 and ₹1,10,000 per eye depending on the procedure type, technology, and city. Standard LASIK runs ₹18,000–₹40,000 per eye nationwide; advanced flapless procedures like SMILE or SILK reach ₹65,000–₹1,10,000 per eye.

### Is LASIK affordable in India?

Yes — India offers some of the best value for LASIK globally. Equivalent procedures cost 3–5x more in the US, UK, or Australia. Even Mumbai's premium pricing at ₹90,000–₹1,10,000 per eye for SILK or SMILE is competitive on a global basis. Patients who wear contact lenses typically recover the LASIK investment within 3.5–5 years through savings on lenses and solutions.

### What is the best city for LASIK in India?

Mumbai and Delhi offer the highest concentration of experienced refractive surgeons and the widest range of laser platforms. For most patients in Maharashtra, Mumbai is the practical choice — though Bangalore, Hyderabad, and Chennai all have excellent clinics for local patients. The surgeon and screening quality matter more than the city itself.

### Can I get LASIK in India for under ₹20,000 per eye?

Yes, but understand what you are getting: standard microkeratome LASIK for straightforward prescriptions at moderate-volume clinics, often excluding pre-op tests and medications. This can be entirely appropriate for the right candidate — but only after thorough screening confirms suitability, which itself adds to the cost.

### Does insurance cover LASIK in India?

Standard health insurance policies in India treat LASIK as an elective cosmetic procedure and do not cover it. A small number of employer benefit plans offer partial reimbursement — check your HR benefits documentation. No-cost EMI financing through providers like Bajaj Finserv or HDFC is available at most major clinics for 3–24 months.
