# SILK LASIK in Mumbai: The Newest Bladeless Procedure Explained

SILK LASIK in Mumbai is the newest flapless laser vision correction procedure available — and patients are right to be curious. Launched in India around 2022–2023, SILK builds on the same core concept as SMILE (no flap, no corneal surface disruption), but uses a different laser platform that its proponents claim produces smoother lenticule edges and a marginally faster early recovery.

If you are evaluating SILK alongside SMILE, Contoura, and Femto LASIK, this guide gives you an honest, complete picture — including what the technology actually does, what outcomes you can realistically expect, what it costs at Mumbai clinics in 2026, and the one significant caveat every patient should know before choosing it.

---

## What Is SILK LASIK?

SILK stands for **Smooth Incision Lenticule Keratomileusis**. Like SMILE, it is a flapless procedure — meaning no corneal flap is ever created. Instead, a laser creates a lenticule (a small disc of corneal tissue) inside the cornea, which is then removed through a tiny incision.

The key difference between SILK and SMILE is the **laser platform** used to create the lenticule.

- **SMILE** uses the Carl Zeiss VisuMax femtosecond laser, which has been in clinical use since 2007.
- **SILK** uses a solid-state femtosecond laser system — such as the Ellex/Ziemer FEMTO LDV Z8 or similar platform — which operates with shorter pulse durations and claims to produce a smoother lenticule surface.

In practical terms: both procedures go inside your cornea without touching the surface, extract a lenticule, and correct your myopia. The theoretical advantage of SILK is the quality of the lenticule edges — cleaner, smoother cuts may translate to a faster visual recovery and potentially better early optical quality. However, this advantage is based on early clinical reports, not decades of large-scale data.

## The SILK Procedure: What Happens in the Laser Room

The SILK procedure is similar to SMILE in its broad outline. Here is what to expect at a Mumbai clinic offering SILK:

### Step 1: Pre-operative Assessment

Corneal topography, pachymetry (thickness measurement), pupillometry, and wavefront analysis are performed. These tests confirm whether you are a suitable candidate and define the lenticule dimensions. Expect 60–90 minutes for the full workup.

### Step 2: Preparation

You are taken to the laser suite. Anaesthetic eye drops are applied — you remain fully awake but feel no pain. Your eye area is cleaned and a small speculum holds your eyelids gently open.

### Step 3: Docking and Laser Application

Your eye is docked to the laser system with gentle suction. The solid-state femtosecond laser creates two curved cuts inside the corneal stroma (the middle layer of your cornea) — defining the top and bottom surfaces of the lenticule. The brief suction phase typically lasts 20–30 seconds per eye.

### Step 4: Lenticule Extraction

Your surgeon uses fine micro-instruments to access the lenticule through the small incision and carefully separate and remove it. The precision and gentleness of this step are among the most technically demanding aspects of the procedure — which is why surgical experience matters enormously.

### Step 5: Post-procedure Care

No flap means no patch over the eye. Antibiotic and anti-inflammatory drops are prescribed. Vision is initially hazy; you rest for a few hours before going home. A follow-up is scheduled for the next morning.

## Who Is a Good SILK Candidate?

SILK is appropriate for:

- **Myopes (short-sighted patients)** with prescriptions typically between −1.00 and −10.00 D
- **Patients with myopic astigmatism** (most platforms treat up to −3.00 to −5.00 D of astigmatism)
- **Patients with dry eye concerns** who want to avoid the nerve disruption of a flap-based procedure
- **Athletes and contact sport players** who cannot risk a flap
- **Patients who want the latest technology** and whose surgeon has the specific SILK platform and appropriate training

SILK is **not suitable** for:

- Hyperopic (farsighted) patients — SILK corrects only myopia and myopic astigmatism
- Patients with corneal thickness below the safe threshold
- Patients with active corneal disease (keratoconus, pellucid marginal degeneration, etc.)
- Patients requiring topography-guided treatment for significant higher-order aberrations — Contoura LASIK is more appropriate in those cases

## What "Solid-State Laser" Means and Why It Matters

Understanding the technology helps you ask better questions at your consultation.

Conventional femtosecond lasers (like the VisuMax used in SMILE) use a **diode-pumped mode-locked laser** to generate ultra-short pulses of infrared light. Each pulse creates a tiny bubble of plasma in the corneal tissue — thousands of these bubble spots together form the lenticule boundary.

**Solid-state femtosecond lasers** (used in SILK platforms) operate with even shorter pulse durations — sometimes in the femtosecond to low-picosecond range — and produce smaller, more tightly controlled photodisruption zones. The claimed result: the tissue planes created between the lenticule and surrounding cornea are smoother, with less micro-roughness at the cut surface.

Why does this matter clinically? Smoother lenticule surfaces may mean:

- **Better optical quality** during the healing phase — less light scatter from rough tissue interfaces
- **Slightly faster visual recovery** as the cornea adapts more quickly to the new shape
- **Potentially less intra-operative stress** on surrounding corneal tissue

The key word is "may." These theoretical advantages are biologically plausible and supported by early short-term data — but they have not yet been confirmed by the kind of large-scale, long-term trials that validate SMILE or LASIK.

## SILK Results: Early Outcome Data

SILK is too new to have the 10–15 year follow-up studies that define SMILE's or LASIK's evidence base. What exists is promising but limited:

- Early studies from India and Europe (2022–2024) report **95%+ of patients achieving 20/20 or better** at three and six months post-operatively.
- Visual recovery timelines in early case series show many patients reaching functional clarity within **2–4 days** — marginally faster than the typical 3–7 day SMILE timeline.
- Dry eye outcomes appear comparable to SMILE — significantly better than flap-based LASIK.
- Refractive predictability (how closely the achieved correction matches the targeted correction) appears equivalent to SMILE in the short term.

The honest summary: the outcomes data available today suggests SILK is safe and effective. However, surgeons at Mumbai's most established refractive centres remain cautious about recommending SILK over SMILE without stronger long-term data to confirm equivalent stability and safety over 5–10 years.

## Is SILK Better Than SMILE? An Honest Assessment

This is the question patients ask most often — and it deserves a direct, balanced answer.

**The case for SILK:**
- Theoretically smoother lenticule edges from the solid-state laser
- Early reports suggest slightly faster initial visual recovery
- Flapless — same biomechanical and dry eye advantages as SMILE

**The case for SMILE:**
- 15+ years of published outcome data from tens of thousands of cases globally
- Proven long-term stability and safety record
- Available at more Mumbai centres with more surgeons experienced in the technique
- Strong enhancement protocol established over many years of practice

**The honest verdict:** SMILE is the more proven procedure by a wide margin. If your surgeon is highly experienced with SMILE and the clinic has a strong SMILE track record, choosing SILK primarily for the "newest technology" advantage is not clinically justified given current evidence.

SILK may be worth considering if your surgeon specifically recommends it based on your corneal characteristics, or if you are being treated at one of Mumbai's few early-adopter centres with genuine SILK expertise and a transparent outcomes register.

In either case, the most important variable is not SILK vs SMILE — it is the skill and experience of the surgeon performing the procedure.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is SILK LASIK?

SILK (Smooth Incision Lenticule Keratomileusis) is the newest flapless laser eye surgery procedure. Like SMILE, it creates a lenticule inside the cornea and removes it through a small incision. The difference is the laser platform — SILK uses a solid-state femtosecond laser that claims smoother lenticule edges compared to the VisuMax used in SMILE.

### SILK vs SMILE LASIK: which is better?

SMILE has 15+ years of large-scale clinical evidence confirming its safety and effectiveness. SILK has only 2–3 years of limited data. Both are flapless procedures with similar advantages over LASIK for dry eye and contact sports. For most patients today, SMILE is the more evidence-backed choice. SILK may prove equivalent or superior over time as more data accumulates.

### Is SILK LASIK available in Mumbai?

Yes, SILK LASIK is available at a small number of specialist refractive surgery centres in Mumbai. Because it requires a specific laser platform and specialised training, it is not as widely available as SMILE or Femto LASIK.

### What is SILK LASIK cost in India?

SILK LASIK costs ₹90,000–₹1,10,000 per eye at Mumbai clinics in 2026. For both eyes, the total package is typically ₹1,80,000–₹2,20,000. This makes it the highest-cost laser vision correction option currently available.
