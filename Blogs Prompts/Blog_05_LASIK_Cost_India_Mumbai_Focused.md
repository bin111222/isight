# What Is the Cost of LASIK Eye Surgery in India? (Mumbai Patient's Guide)

The cost of LASIK eye surgery in India in 2026 ranges from approximately ₹40,000 to ₹2,20,000 for both eyes — a wide band that depends on the procedure type, the laser technology used, the surgeon's experience, and the city. If you are a Mumbai patient trying to understand what you will actually pay and why prices vary so dramatically, this guide gives you an honest, complete breakdown with no marketing spin.

---

## The Honest National Cost Range for LASIK in India (2026)

Before diving into the details, here is the realistic national cost range for a complete LASIK treatment episode — meaning the procedure plus pre-op workup, medications, and follow-up visits — for both eyes:

- **Standard LASIK:** ₹45,000–₹90,000 both eyes (all-in)
- **Bladeless / Femto LASIK:** ₹1,15,000–₹1,80,000 both eyes (all-in)
- **Contoura Vision:** ₹1,55,000–₹2,10,000 both eyes (all-in)
- **SMILE LASIK:** ₹1,65,000–₹2,20,000 both eyes (all-in)
- **SILK LASIK:** ₹1,85,000–₹2,25,000 both eyes (all-in)
- **Trans-PRK:** ₹85,000–₹1,45,000 both eyes (all-in)

These figures include the most common add-ons (medications, basic follow-ups). They do not include enhancement (re-treatment) procedures, which reputable clinics offer free or at a nominal charge within 12 months.

## Why LASIK Prices Vary So Much Within the Same City

Even within Mumbai, you will find identical procedures quoted at vastly different prices. Here are the five main reasons:

### 1. Laser Platform Age and Generation
A clinic running a 2024-vintage VisuMax 800 for SMILE has much higher capital cost than one running a 2017-generation platform. Newer machines deliver faster ablation, smaller spot sizes, and better cyclotorsion tracking — all of which improve outcomes and justify higher fees.

### 2. Surgeon Experience and Specialisation
A surgeon who has performed 15,000+ LASIK procedures with published outcomes data is priced differently from a competent but less experienced practitioner. In Mumbai, the concentration of highly experienced refractive surgeons is high — and patients pay for that depth of expertise.

### 3. Comprehensiveness of Pre-Op Workup
Some clinics perform a full 90–120 minute screening (Pentacam, dry eye battery, wavefront, dilated retina, pachymetry). Others do a brief refraction and corneal topography only. The former costs more to deliver — and protects patients better.

### 4. Inclusions in the Quoted Price
The single biggest source of price confusion is what is "in the quote." A ₹35,000-per-eye quote that excludes pre-op tests, medications, and follow-ups may end up costing more than a ₹45,000-per-eye all-inclusive package.

### 5. Clinic Location and Brand Premium
A clinic in Bandra or Juhu carries higher rent than one in Andheri East or Thane. Brand-name hospital chains also factor in institutional overhead that independent refractive clinics do not have.

## Is There Government or Subsidised LASIK in India?

This is one of the most common questions from patients across India, particularly those from lower-income brackets.

**The honest answer:** There is no national or state government programme providing subsidised LASIK in India as of 2026. LASIK is classified as an elective refractive procedure, not a medical necessity. Government hospitals (AIIMS, regional government eye hospitals) do not routinely offer LASIK — their ophthalmic resources are directed toward medically necessary conditions like cataract surgery for the visually impaired.

Some **medical colleges with attached hospitals** in Maharashtra offer standard LASIK at lower cost (sometimes ₹10,000–₹15,000 per eye), but:
- Waiting times are typically long (weeks to months)
- Procedures may be performed by residents under supervision
- Laser platforms may be older generation
- Post-op support infrastructure is more limited

For patients in Mumbai seeking affordable options, **no-cost EMI** through financing partners is a more practical route than seeking subsidised care.

## How Mumbai Patients Can Get the Best Value

Getting the best value for LASIK in Mumbai does not mean choosing the cheapest quote. It means optimising for the best outcome at a price that is fair and transparent.

Practical steps for Mumbai patients:

1. **Get at least two comprehensive consultations** — not phone quotes; actual pre-op workups. The workup cost (₹1,500–₹3,500) is credited back at most clinics, making this essentially free if you proceed.

2. **Ask about procedure suitability honestly.** If a clinic recommends Contoura or SMILE for your straightforward −2.50D prescription with normal corneas, ask why — standard Femto LASIK may deliver equivalent results at lower cost.

3. **Compare all-in quotes in writing.** Total the complete treatment episode across clinics, not just the procedure line item.

4. **Factor in the enhancement policy.** A free lifetime enhancement guarantee has genuine financial value — especially for prescriptions at the higher end of the correctable range where minor residual errors are more common.

5. **Use EMI wisely.** No-cost EMI on ₹1,50,000 over 12 months costs ₹12,500 per month — manageable for most Mumbai households. Avoid personal loans at high interest rates to fund LASIK.

## FAQ

### What is the cost of LASIK in India?

LASIK costs ₹18,000–₹1,10,000 per eye in India in 2026, depending on procedure type and city. For both eyes, the total ranges from ₹40,000 (standard LASIK, budget centre) to ₹2,20,000 (SMILE or SILK at a premium clinic). Mumbai prices are at the upper end nationally, reflecting higher surgeon experience and equipment quality.

### Is LASIK expensive in India?

Relative to global benchmarks, LASIK in India is very affordable. The same SMILE procedure that costs ₹80,000–₹1,05,000 per eye in Mumbai would cost ₹2,50,000–₹4,00,000 per eye in the UK or ₹3,00,000–₹5,00,000 per eye in the US. Even at Mumbai's premium end, India offers exceptional value for internationally benchmarked surgical quality.

### What does LASIK for both eyes cost in India?

Both-eyes LASIK pricing is typically 10% less than double the per-eye price. At a mid-tier Mumbai clinic doing Bladeless LASIK, expect ₹1,00,000–₹1,50,000 for both eyes as an all-in figure. Premium flapless procedures (SMILE, SILK) at top Mumbai clinics run ₹1,60,000–₹2,20,000 for both eyes.

### Are there hidden costs in LASIK in India?

Yes, at some clinics. The items most often excluded from headline prices are: pre-op screening workup, post-op medications, follow-up consultation fees, and enhancement procedures. Always ask for a complete written breakdown of the full treatment episode before comparing prices.

### Can I do LASIK on a budget in India?

Yes. Standard LASIK is clinically appropriate for many patients with moderate, stable prescriptions and normal corneas. At a reputable clinic in Mumbai, standard LASIK costs ₹20,000–₹40,000 per eye. The key is thorough pre-op screening — do not skip the workup to save money, as this is where candidacy and safety are determined.
