## There Is No Single "Best" LASIK - There's the Best Fit

Before comparing procedures, understand the principle that separates good clinics from sales-driven ones: **the best procedure is matched to your eyes after a full work-up, not chosen from a price list.** A clinic that recommends the same procedure to every patient - or quotes a price before examining you - is not individualising care.

Three measurements drive the decision more than anything else:

- **Corneal thickness** - thin corneas rule out some procedures and favour flapless options.
- **Prescription type and strength** - high myopia, hyperopia, and astigmatism each behave differently.
- **Tear film / dry eye status** - significant dry eye favours nerve-sparing procedures.

## Which Procedure Is Best For You? A Quick Guide

### If you have dry eyes or are dry-eye-prone
**SMILE** is usually the strongest option. Because it's flapless and preserves more corneal nerves, post-operative dry eye is significantly less common and milder.

### If you have high myopia (up to about −10 D)
**SMILE** again often leads, as it removes less tissue and preserves corneal strength. For very high powers beyond LASIK/SMILE limits, your surgeon may recommend an **ICL** (implantable lens) instead.

### If you have significant astigmatism or want the sharpest night vision
**Contoura Vision** is frequently the best choice. Its topography-guided treatment maps 22,000 points and can smooth irregularities that standard treatments ignore - drivers and night workers often notice the difference.

### If you're farsighted (hyperopia)
**SMILE is not an option** for hyperopia. LASIK (standard, Femto, or Contoura) or a lens-based procedure is the route.

### If you're on a tighter budget with a healthy, thick cornea and modest power
**Standard or Femto LASIK** delivers excellent, proven results at a lower cost. Femto adds bladeless flap creation for a measurably lower flap-complication rate.

### If you want the newest technology and a smooth recovery
**SILK** is the latest flapless platform, designed for a gentle healing curve - discuss availability and suitability with your surgeon.

## What Makes a Result Genuinely "Best"

The best LASIK outcome isn't just 20/20 on a chart the next morning - it's a stable, comfortable, high-quality result over years. That depends on:

1. **A thorough pre-op work-up** - Pentacam topography, pachymetry, dry eye testing, wavefront aberrometry, pupil measurement, and a dilated retina check.
2. **The right procedure choice** - matched to your eyes, not the clinic's inventory.
3. **An experienced refractive surgeon** - ask how many of *that* procedure they personally perform.
4. **Modern, well-maintained laser platforms** - and ideally more than one, so the surgeon can recommend honestly.
5. **Structured follow-up** - Day 1, Week 1, Month 1, and Month 3 at minimum.

## FAQ

### Which is the best LASIK surgery in Mumbai?

There's no universal best. The best procedure is the one matched to your corneal thickness, prescription, dry eye status, and lifestyle after a full work-up. SMILE often suits dry-eye-prone high myopes, Contoura suits significant astigmatism and night-vision quality, and Standard/Femto LASIK suit healthy corneas with modest power.

### Is SMILE better than LASIK?

For dry-eye-prone patients, high myopes, and contact-sport players, SMILE is often preferable because it's flapless and gentler on corneal nerves. But SMILE cannot correct hyperopia, and Contoura LASIK may give better optical quality for significant astigmatism. The "better" option depends on your eyes.

### How much does the best LASIK in Mumbai cost?

Per eye in 2026: Standard LASIK ₹20,000–₹40,000, Femto ₹55,000–₹85,000, Contoura ₹75,000–₹1,00,000, SMILE ₹80,000–₹1,05,000, and SILK ₹90,000–₹1,10,000. Both eyes usually cost roughly double, often with a small package discount.

### How do I know which LASIK procedure I qualify for?

Only a full pre-operative assessment can tell you. It measures corneal thickness, maps the cornea, tests your tear film, and confirms your prescription stability - together these determine which procedures are safe and best for you.

### Is the most expensive LASIK always the best?

No. The newest or priciest procedure isn't automatically right for your eyes. A well-chosen Femto LASIK can outperform a costlier option that doesn't suit your prescription or corneal profile. Fit and surgeon experience matter more than price.
