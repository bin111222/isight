# SMILE LASIK Surgery in Mumbai: Is It Better Than Standard LASIK?

SMILE LASIK surgery in Mumbai is now one of the most requested vision correction procedures in the city — and for good reason. Unlike standard LASIK, which creates a corneal flap, SMILE is entirely flapless. A tiny 2–4mm incision is all that is made in your eye. The result: less disruption to corneal nerves, significantly lower rates of post-operative dry eye, and no flap that could theoretically shift if you take a hit to the eye during contact sports.

But SMILE is not the right choice for everyone. This guide gives you the complete, honest picture — including who SMILE helps most, who it does not suit, what it costs at clinics in Mumbai in 2026, and how it compares to standard LASIK and Femto LASIK.

---

## What Is SMILE LASIK?

SMILE stands for **Small Incision Lenticule Extraction**. Despite often being called "SMILE LASIK," it is technically a distinct procedure from LASIK — because no flap is ever created.

Here is how it works:

A femtosecond laser fires rapid pulses inside your cornea (not on its surface) and creates a precise disc of corneal tissue called a **lenticule**. This lenticule — shaped and sized to match your exact prescription — is then extracted through a small 2–4mm incision on the corneal surface.

When the lenticule is removed, the remaining corneal tissue reshapes slightly, correcting your refractive error. No flap. No excimer laser. The corneal surface is virtually untouched.

Compare this to standard LASIK, where a 20mm circular flap is cut across the corneal surface, folded back, and an excimer laser ablates tissue underneath. The flap is then repositioned. The incision in SMILE is roughly **five to ten times smaller** than a LASIK flap.

### Why Does "No Flap" Matter?

The corneal flap in LASIK severs many of the surface nerve fibres responsible for triggering tear production. This is why dry eye is the most common complaint after LASIK — particularly in the first 3–6 months. SMILE preserves the majority of these corneal nerve fibres because the procedure happens inside the cornea rather than on its surface. Studies consistently show **lower rates and severity of dry eye** after SMILE compared to LASIK.

Additionally, a LASIK flap — although it heals strongly — does remain a potential weak point if the eye suffers blunt trauma. SMILE carries no such structural concern.

## Who Is SMILE Ideal For?

### People with Chronic Dry Eyes

If you already suffer from dry eye disease or marginal tear film quality, SMILE is strongly preferred over LASIK. The minimal nerve disruption means your eyes are far less likely to feel gritty, scratchy, or uncomfortable after surgery. This is one of the clearest clinical advantages SMILE holds over any flap-based procedure.

### High Myopia (up to −10.00 D)

Standard LASIK has corneal thickness limitations for very high prescriptions. SMILE can safely treat myopia up to approximately −10.00 D while removing less overall corneal tissue and maintaining better biomechanical strength. If your prescription is in the −6.00 to −10.00 range, SMILE is often the first recommendation from experienced refractive surgeons in Mumbai.

### Athletes and Contact Sport Players

A LASIK flap, once created, never fully adheres with the same strength as native corneal tissue. A hard impact to the eye — even years after surgery — could theoretically displace the flap. For anyone who plays combat sports, football, cricket, martial arts, or any activity involving physical contact or impact risk, SMILE is the significantly safer choice.

### Patients Who Want a Minimally Invasive Approach

The smaller incision, minimal surface disruption, and preserved corneal architecture make SMILE appealing to patients who are cautious about surgery and want the least invasive effective option.

## Who Is SMILE NOT Suitable For?

### Patients with Hyperopia (Farsightedness)

This is the most important limitation of SMILE: **it cannot correct hyperopia**. SMILE is approved only for myopia (short-sightedness) and myopic astigmatism. If you are farsighted, you will need LASIK, PRK/Trans-PRK, or a lens-based procedure instead.

### Patients Requiring Topography-Guided Treatment

SMILE does not currently have the same topography-guided, personalised ablation capability as Contoura Vision LASIK. If your surgeon finds significant corneal irregularities or higher-order aberrations that need targeted correction, Contoura LASIK may deliver better optical quality despite involving a flap.

### Corneas Below the Minimum Thickness Threshold

Like all laser procedures, SMILE requires adequate corneal tissue. Patients with very thin corneas (below approximately 480–490 microns) may not have sufficient tissue for safe SMILE treatment. Your surgeon's pre-operative assessment will determine this.

### Mixed Refractive Errors Requiring Hyperopic Correction

Any prescription that includes a hyperopic component cannot be treated with SMILE. Your full refraction data will clarify this at your consultation.

## Step-by-Step SMILE Procedure in Mumbai

### Step 1: Pre-operative Assessment (1–2 Hours)

Corneal topography, pachymetry (corneal thickness measurement), wavefront analysis, and pupil dilation measurements are performed. These maps determine whether you are a SMILE candidate and define the lenticule parameters.

### Step 2: Anaesthetic Eye Drops

Numbing drops are instilled. You are awake throughout the procedure. Most patients report feeling no pain — only mild pressure.

### Step 3: Femtosecond Laser Phase (Approximately 25–35 Seconds Per Eye)

You lie under the laser and fix your gaze on a blinking target light. The femtosecond laser creates the lenticule and the small access incision. You may notice a brief dimming of the light — this is normal and indicates the laser is working.

### Step 4: Lenticule Extraction (1–2 Minutes Per Eye)

Your surgeon uses fine instruments to gently separate and extract the lenticule through the 2–4mm incision. This is the most technically demanding part of the procedure and is where your surgeon's experience matters most.

### Step 5: Recovery Begins

No patch is required. Your vision will be hazy immediately after surgery. Most Mumbai patients are comfortable within a few hours and notice dramatic improvement by the next morning.

## SMILE Recovery Timeline

SMILE's recovery pattern is slightly different from LASIK — and it is important to have realistic expectations.

| Timeframe | What to Expect |
|---|---|
| First 2–4 hours | Hazy, light-sensitive vision; mild watering |
| Day 1 (24 hours) | Noticeably improved but still soft focus for many patients |
| Days 2–3 | Most patients see reasonably well; some residual blurriness normal |
| Days 3–7 | Vision sharpens progressively; most reach functional clarity |
| 2–4 weeks | Vision continues to refine; final quality nearly established |
| 4–8 weeks | Full stabilisation; final refraction check recommended |

The slightly slower initial clarity (versus LASIK's 24-hour result) is inherent to the SMILE healing pattern. The flapless cornea heals from the inside out, which takes a few extra days. This is not a sign of any problem — it is simply how the procedure works. By the end of the first week, the vast majority of SMILE patients in Mumbai have vision that is functionally excellent.

## SMILE Results: 15 Years of Long-Term Evidence

SMILE is not a new or experimental procedure. The first human SMILE surgeries were performed in 2007, and the procedure has been studied extensively over 15 years.

**What the evidence shows:**

- **95–98% of patients achieve 20/20 or better** uncorrected distance vision at one year.
- Long-term studies (5–10 years) show **equivalent refractive outcomes to LASIK** for myopia correction.
- SMILE patients consistently report **better dry eye scores** post-operatively compared to LASIK patients.
- Corneal biomechanical strength is better preserved with SMILE, which has implications for long-term stability.
- Enhancement rates (re-treatment) are comparable to LASIK at approximately 1–3%.

One important note: because SMILE treats the cornea from the inside, enhancements (if needed) are performed differently — typically via surface ablation (Trans-PRK) rather than a repeat SMILE or flap lift. Discuss your clinic's enhancement policy before surgery.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is SMILE better than LASIK?

For the right patient, yes. SMILE is better than LASIK for patients with dry eye tendencies, high myopia, and those who play contact sports. LASIK (particularly Contoura) may deliver better optical quality for patients with significant higher-order aberrations. The "better" procedure depends entirely on your individual corneal maps, prescription, and lifestyle.

### SMILE LASIK vs Contoura: which should I choose?

SMILE and Contoura address different concerns. SMILE eliminates the flap and greatly reduces dry eye. Contoura optimises optical quality using personalised topography mapping. Some patients are better served by one, some by the other. A small number of clinics in Mumbai now offer topography-guided lenticule extraction, which aims to combine both advantages — ask your surgeon if this is available.

### Does SMILE LASIK cause dry eyes?

SMILE causes significantly less post-operative dry eye than LASIK because corneal nerves are largely preserved. Mild temporary dryness can still occur in the first few weeks, but it is typically far less troublesome than after LASIK.

### What is the SMILE LASIK cost in Mumbai?

SMILE LASIK costs ₹80,000–₹1,05,000 per eye at reputable centres in Mumbai in 2026. Both eyes together typically cost ₹1,60,000–₹2,10,000 including follow-up.
