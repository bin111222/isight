## What Is LASIK Eye Surgery?

LASIK stands for **Laser-Assisted In Situ Keratomileusis**. It's a refractive surgery that corrects nearsightedness (myopia), farsightedness (hyperopia) and astigmatism by precisely reshaping the cornea — the clear, dome-shaped window at the front of your eye — so that light focuses correctly on the retina instead of in front of or behind it.

LASIK has been performed on **millions of patients worldwide since the early 1990s** and is one of the most studied elective procedures in medicine. Modern LASIK in Mumbai is a world apart from the first-generation surgery: today's lasers track your eye 500–1,000 times per second, and the entire refractive step takes seconds rather than minutes.

## How LASIK Works in Mumbai: The Procedure Step by Step

Here's what actually happens during LASIK at a modern Mumbai refractive centre. You'll be awake, you'll be fine, and the part that sounds scariest (the flap) is over in under 20 seconds per eye.

### Step 1 — Numbing drops
Anaesthetic eye drops are instilled. Within 30 seconds your eye is completely numb, though you still see movement and light. You are **awake and breathing normally throughout** — LASIK does not use general anaesthesia.

### Step 2 — Eyelid holder
A small device gently holds your eyelids open so you can't blink during the brief laser steps. This feels mildly unusual but is painless.

### Step 3 — Flap creation
This is the main variable between LASIK procedures:
- **Standard LASIK:** A microkeratome (a precision oscillating blade) creates a thin hinged corneal flap.
- **Bladeless / Femto-LASIK, Contoura, SILK:** A femtosecond laser creates the flap — no blade touches the eye. Takes ~15 seconds per eye.
- **SMILE:** No flap at all. A femtosecond laser creates a tiny lenticule inside the cornea, which is removed through a 2–4mm keyhole incision.

You'll feel slight pressure for about 20 seconds. Your vision dims briefly — this is normal.

### Step 4 — The excimer laser (the "correction")
The flap is gently lifted, and the excimer laser reshapes the corneal tissue underneath according to a personalised map of your eye. **The laser is on for only 10–40 seconds per eye**, depending on your prescription. You'll hear a tick-tick sound and smell a faint burnt-hair odour (this is normal — it's the laser interacting with air).

The eye-tracking system follows your gaze 500–1,000 times a second, so tiny movements don't affect precision.

### Step 5 — Flap replacement
The flap is repositioned naturally. No stitches, no bandage. It re-adheres by molecular suction within minutes.

### Step 6 — Same thing on the second eye
Usually done immediately, one eye after the other.

**Total time in the laser room:** 10–15 minutes. **Total time at the clinic:** 90 minutes to 3 hours, including pre-op prep, post-op check, and eye-drop demonstration.

## Types of LASIK Offered in Mumbai (and How to Choose)

Not all LASIK is the same. Mumbai clinics now offer up to six different refractive procedures. Here's a plain-English comparison:

| Procedure | How It Works | Who It's Best For | Recovery | Mumbai Cost (per eye) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard LASIK** | Blade flap + excimer laser | Low-moderate power, healthy corneas, budget | Fast | ₹20,000–₹40,000 |
| **Femto / Bladeless LASIK** | Laser flap + excimer laser | Thinner corneas, precision seekers | Fast | ₹55,000–₹85,000 |
| **Contoura Vision** | Topography-guided laser using a 22,000-point corneal map | Higher prescriptions, irregular corneas, night drivers | Fast | ₹75,000–₹1,00,000 |
| **SMILE** | Flapless — lenticule extracted through a keyhole incision | High myopia, dry-eye prone, athletes/military | Moderate | ₹80,000–₹1,05,000 |
| **SILK** | Newest flapless solid-state laser platform | Latest-tech seekers, smooth recovery priority | Fast | ₹90,000–₹1,10,000 |
| **Trans-PRK** | No flap — laser applied directly to corneal surface | Very thin corneas, contact sports, patients ineligible for LASIK | Slow (7–10 days) | ₹40,000–₹70,000 |

**Quick decision guide:**
- Low myopia (< −4.00 D), thick cornea, normal topography → **Standard or Femto LASIK**
- High myopia (> −6.00 D) or dry eyes → consider **SMILE**
- Significant astigmatism or want the sharpest night vision → **Contoura Vision**
- Want the newest platform with least post-op discomfort → **SILK**
- Borderline thin cornea or contact athlete → **Trans-PRK**

This decision can only be made confidently after a proper pre-op assessment. A good Mumbai refractive surgeon will recommend the procedure that suits *your* eyes — not the most expensive one on the menu.

## Who Is a Good Candidate for LASIK in Mumbai?

LASIK is a brilliant procedure — for the right eye. Here's who is typically a good candidate, and who isn't.

### You are likely a good candidate if:
- You are **18 years or older** (ideally 21+)
- Your **prescription has been stable for at least 12 months** (no change in spectacle power)
- You have **myopia up to −10.00 D, hyperopia up to +4.00 D, or astigmatism up to −5.00 D** (varies by procedure)
- Your **corneal thickness is sufficient** (typically > 480 microns post-flap residual stromal bed)
- Your **eyes are healthy** — no keratoconus, advanced dry eye, uncontrolled glaucoma, or active infection
- You are **not pregnant or breastfeeding** (pregnancy shifts refraction temporarily)
- You have **realistic expectations** — LASIK aims to reduce spectacle dependence, not to produce superhuman vision

### LASIK may not be for you if:
- You have **keratoconus or corneal ectasia**, or a family history of either
- Your **cornea is too thin** for the procedure you want
- You have **severe dry eye syndrome** (some cases can be pre-treated; many cannot)
- You have **autoimmune disease** (rheumatoid arthritis, lupus) that may slow corneal healing
- You have **uncontrolled diabetes**
- Your **prescription is still changing** — common under age 21 or during pregnancy

**Being told you're not a LASIK candidate is not bad news — it's your surgeon protecting you.** Mumbai patients told they're unsuitable for LASIK often qualify for ICL (Implantable Collamer Lens) or refractive lens exchange instead.

## Pre-LASIK Assessment: What Happens Before Surgery

Before any laser touches your eye, a comprehensive **pre-operative workup** is mandatory. Skipping this is the single biggest red flag in LASIK care. At a reputable Mumbai clinic, expect the following tests across 90–120 minutes:

- **Visual acuity** (uncorrected and corrected)
- **Cycloplegic refraction** — a more accurate "true prescription" test done after dilating drops
- **Corneal topography** (Pentacam or similar) — maps the entire corneal surface
- **Pachymetry** — measures corneal thickness
- **Aberrometry / wavefront** — maps higher-order optical errors
- **Tear film / dry eye assessment** (Schirmer's test, TBUT)
- **Pupillometry** — pupil size under dim light
- **Dilated retinal examination** — rules out any back-of-eye issues
- **Intraocular pressure**
- **Detailed medical history** — medications, systemic conditions, previous eye issues

If the clinic's "pre-op" takes under 30 minutes, it is not a proper LASIK workup. Walk away.

You'll also be told to **stop wearing contact lenses** before the assessment and surgery:
- Soft lenses: at least 7 days off
- Toric / RGP lenses: at least 2–4 weeks off

## The Day of LASIK Surgery in Mumbai: What to Expect

Most Mumbai LASIK patients arrive in the morning and are home by lunch. Here's the typical timeline:

- **Arrival** — report in loose, comfortable clothing. No perfume, makeup, eye makeup, aftershave or hair spray (they can contaminate the laser).
- **Pre-op prep** (30–45 min) — final checks, cleaning around the eyes, numbing drops, a mild sedative (oral, optional).
- **Laser room** (10–15 min) — the actual procedure.
- **Post-op rest** (30–60 min) — you'll rest with your eyes closed. Some patients doze off.
- **First post-op check** — the surgeon examines the flap position and confirms everything is as expected.
- **Discharge with instructions** — eye drops, eye shield, follow-up appointment.

**You will need someone to take you home.** You can't drive for 24 hours after LASIK. Most Mumbai clinics offer free pickup/drop within city limits.

## LASIK Recovery Timeline: Day-by-Day, Honestly

Here's a realistic picture of what the first month feels like — based on what Mumbai patients actually report, not the glossy version.

### Hours 0–6 (Surgery day)
- Vision is **blurry, like looking through wax paper**. This is normal.
- Eyes **water and sting** for 2–4 hours. Light sensitivity is high.
- **Go home, sleep for 3–4 hours** in a dark room wearing the plastic shield. Sleep is the single best thing you can do.

### Hours 6–24
- On waking, **vision is dramatically clearer** for most patients — often 70–80% of final result.
- Mild gritty sensation ("eyelash in the eye") for another 12 hours.
- **Start eye drops** on the schedule given (typically 4 drops, 4 times a day).

### Day 1 follow-up
- The surgeon checks flap position, cornea clarity, and visual acuity.
- Most patients are **already at 20/20 or 20/25** at this visit.
- Eye shield at night for another 5–7 days to prevent accidental rubbing during sleep.

### Days 2–3
- Most patients **return to work** (desk work, laptop — with 20-20-20 breaks and frequent lubricating drops).
- Vision may fluctuate between morning and evening. This is normal.
- **No water in the eyes** — shower below the neck, no face wash over the eyes.

### Week 1
- Dry, gritty sensation still comes and goes. Use artificial tears **hourly** for the first 7 days; it genuinely helps.
- **Avoid:** dusty areas, swimming pools, beaches, gyms, contact sports, eye makeup.
- **OK:** light walking, commuting, reading, TV.

### Weeks 2–4
- Vision clarity deepens. Night glare and halos (if any) begin fading.
- You can usually resume **mild exercise** by Week 2, and **swimming with goggles** by Week 4 (with your surgeon's approval).
- Eye makeup is usually fine by Week 2.

### Month 1 follow-up
- Refraction confirms final vision.
- Most medications are stopped; lubricants may continue for 1–3 more months.

### Months 2–3
- Vision fully stabilises for the vast majority of patients.
- Any residual dry eye typically resolves in this window.

### Month 6 and beyond
- Final stable vision.
- 6-month follow-up at most Mumbai clinics confirms long-term outcome.

## LASIK Results: What to Realistically Expect

Across the peer-reviewed global literature and Indian outcomes data, here's what LASIK in 2026 delivers:

- **~95% of patients achieve 20/20 vision or better** without glasses
- **99%+ achieve 20/40 or better** — the legal standard for driving without correction
- **1–3%** may need a small enhancement (touch-up) in the first year
- **~5%** report mild dry-eye symptoms beyond 6 months
- **Less than 1%** experience night-time glare or halos that persist beyond 6 months

LASIK does **not** stop you from needing **reading glasses** after age 40–45. That's presbyopia — a separate age-related change. If you're over 40 and considering LASIK, ask your surgeon about **monovision LASIK** or other presbyopia-correcting options.

## Potential Risks and Side Effects (The Honest Version)

We believe every LASIK patient should walk into surgery knowing *exactly* what could go wrong — even if the probability is low. Here's the full list:

**Common, temporary (resolve in days to weeks):**
- Dry eyes
- Night halos or glare (especially in the first 1–3 months)
- Mild light sensitivity
- Fluctuating vision in the first few weeks

**Uncommon, manageable (1–3% of cases):**
- Under-correction or over-correction → treated with a small enhancement
- Persistent dry eye beyond 6 months → treated with lubricants, punctal plugs, or anti-inflammatory drops

**Rare, serious (well under 1%):**
- Flap complications (very rare with femtosecond lasers — under 1 in 10,000)
- Diffuse lamellar keratitis (inflammation under the flap) → treated with steroid drops
- Infection (extremely rare — under 1 in 5,000)
- Corneal ectasia (progressive corneal weakening) — <1 in 2,500 in modern screened patients; this is why pre-op topography is non-negotiable

The single best predictor of a safe outcome is **thorough pre-op screening by an experienced refractive surgeon** — not the specific laser used.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is LASIK painful?
No. Anaesthetic drops completely numb the eye. You'll feel **pressure** for ~20 seconds during flap creation, but no pain. The first 2–4 hours after surgery can feel gritty and watery — this is discomfort, not pain, and it subsides with sleep.

### How long does LASIK surgery in Mumbai take?
The surgery itself takes **10–15 minutes for both eyes**. Your total time at the clinic on surgery day is 90 minutes to 3 hours, including preparation and post-op check. The laser itself runs for under 40 seconds per eye.

### When can I return to work after LASIK in Mumbai?
Most Mumbai patients return to desk work and laptop-based jobs **2–3 days after LASIK**. If your job involves heavy dust (construction, chemicals), high-screen time, or outdoor exposure, discuss a longer break with your surgeon — usually 5–7 days.

### Is LASIK permanent?
The corneal reshaping is permanent. However, your eyes continue to age. After 40–45, you will still likely need **reading glasses** due to presbyopia, which LASIK cannot prevent. About 1–5% of patients may need a small enhancement within 5–10 years as vision drifts slightly.

### Can LASIK be done on both eyes the same day?
Yes — bilateral same-day LASIK is the standard of care in Mumbai and globally. It's safer and more convenient than doing one eye at a time.

### How soon can I use my phone and laptop after LASIK?
By Day 1 or Day 2 most patients can use screens comfortably, provided they take frequent breaks (20-20-20 rule), use lubricating drops every hour, and reduce screen brightness. Full, prolonged screen use is usually comfortable by Day 3–4.

### Can I drive after LASIK?
Do not drive on surgery day. Most patients are cleared to drive 48–72 hours after surgery, but only after the Day 1 check. For night driving, wait until halos have reduced — typically 2–4 weeks.

### Will LASIK cure my reading glasses / will it prevent them later?
LASIK corrects distance vision. It does not prevent presbyopia (age-related near-vision change) that typically starts between 40 and 45. Some Mumbai patients over 40 choose **monovision LASIK** — one eye corrected for distance, one for near — to delay reading glasses.

### Is LASIK safe in Mumbai specifically?
Yes — Mumbai hosts many of India's most experienced refractive surgeons and the latest laser platforms (Visumax 800, WaveLight Plus, Teneo M2). Safety depends far more on **surgeon experience + pre-op screening** than on city.

### What happens if I blink or move during LASIK?
You can't blink — an eyelid holder prevents it. For movement, the laser's eye-tracking system adjusts in real time 500–1,000 times a second. Even if you move your eye slightly, the laser compensates. If you make a very large movement, the laser simply pauses until your eye is in position.

### How long do LASIK results last?
LASIK results are stable and long-lasting for the great majority of patients. Long-term studies (15+ years) show most patients retain their corrected vision. Some drift may occur due to natural ageing — the fix is a small enhancement, which is straightforward.

### What is the success rate of LASIK in Mumbai?
When performed by an experienced surgeon on a properly screened patient, LASIK has a **success rate above 95%** for achieving 20/20 vision and above **99% for achieving 20/40** (driving-standard vision). Complications are rare; serious complications are very rare.
