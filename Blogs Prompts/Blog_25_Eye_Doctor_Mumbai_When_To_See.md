## When Should You See an Eye Doctor?

You should book an eye doctor in Mumbai if you notice any of the following - and some of these need same-day attention.

### See a doctor soon (within days to weeks)
- Gradual blurring of near or distance vision
- Frequent headaches or eye strain, especially with screen work
- Persistent dry, gritty, burning, or watering eyes
- Difficulty with night driving or increasing glare
- Needing brighter light to read than before
- It's simply been over 1–2 years since your last check

### See a doctor urgently (same day)
- **Sudden loss of vision** in one or both eyes
- **A curtain or shadow** moving across your vision
- **A sudden shower of floaters** or flashes of light
- **Severe eye pain** with redness, nausea, or halos around lights
- **Eye injury** or chemical splash
- Sudden double vision or a drooping eyelid

Sudden symptoms can signal retinal detachment, acute glaucoma, or other sight-threatening emergencies. When in doubt, treat it as urgent.

## Who Should Get Regular Eye Check-Ups (Even Without Symptoms)?

Many sight-threatening conditions - glaucoma, diabetic retinopathy, early macular changes - cause no symptoms until they are advanced. Routine screening catches them early.

| Group | Recommended frequency |
|---|---|
| Adults 18–39, no risk factors | Every 2 years |
| Adults 40+ | Every 1–2 years |
| People with diabetes | At least once a year (dilated retina exam) |
| High spectacle power / family history of eye disease | Yearly |
| Glaucoma suspects or on glaucoma treatment | As advised, usually 2–4 times a year |
| Children | Before school, then as advised; any time a concern appears |

If you have diabetes, an annual dilated retina examination is one of the most important appointments you can keep - diabetic retinopathy is a leading cause of preventable blindness and is highly manageable when caught early.

## What Happens at an Eye Doctor Visit in Mumbai

Knowing the flow removes the anxiety. A comprehensive visit typically includes:

1. **History** - your symptoms, medical conditions, medications, and family history.
2. **Vision test (visual acuity)** - reading a chart with and without glasses.
3. **Refraction** - fine-tuning your exact glasses prescription.
4. **Slit-lamp exam** - a magnified look at the lids, cornea, and lens.
5. **Eye pressure check** - to screen for glaucoma.
6. **Dilated retina exam** - drops widen the pupil so the retina and optic nerve can be assessed (your vision will be blurry and light-sensitive for a few hours afterwards - arrange not to drive).
7. **Additional scans if needed** - OCT, corneal topography, or visual field testing.

A full visit with dilation usually takes 45–90 minutes. Bring your current glasses, a list of medications, and dark sunglasses for the journey home.

## How Much Does an Eye Doctor Cost in Mumbai? (2026)

| Service | Typical Mumbai range |
|---|---|
| General consultation | ₹500 – ₹1,200 |
| Senior / specialist consultation | ₹1,000 – ₹2,500 |

Fees are higher in South Mumbai and Bandra and lower in the suburbs and Navi Mumbai. Many clinics credit a pre-surgical evaluation fee toward your procedure if you go ahead. Always confirm what the consultation fee includes before you book.

## How to Choose the Right Eye Doctor in Mumbai

- **Match the doctor to your problem.** Eye care is sub-specialised - refractive, cataract, retina, glaucoma, paediatric, cornea/dry eye, and oculoplasty are distinct fields.
- **Check qualifications.** Look for MS/MD Ophthalmology, ideally with a fellowship in the relevant area.
- **Look for modern diagnostics on-site** (OCT, topography, fundus imaging).
- **Read genuine reviews** - 4.5+ ratings with specific, detailed feedback.
- **Value clear communication** - a good doctor explains what's wrong and why in plain language.
- **Avoid same-day surgery pressure** and vague, non-itemised quotes.

## FAQ

### When should I see an eye doctor in Mumbai?

See one promptly for blurring, persistent eye strain, dry or watering eyes, night-driving difficulty, or if it's been over 1–2 years since your last exam. Seek same-day care for sudden vision loss, a curtain or shadow over vision, sudden floaters or flashes, severe eye pain, or any eye injury.

### How much does an eye doctor consultation cost in Mumbai?

A general consultation typically costs ₹500–₹1,200, a senior or specialist consultation ₹1,000–₹2,500, and a comprehensive exam with diagnostics ₹2,000–₹5,000. Costs are higher in South Mumbai and Bandra than in the suburbs.

### How often should I get my eyes checked?

Every 2 years if you're under 40 with no risk factors, every 1–2 years after 40, and at least once a year if you have diabetes, high power, glaucoma, or a family history of eye disease.

### Will my eyes be dilated at the appointment?

Often yes - dilation lets the doctor examine your retina and optic nerve. Your vision will be blurry and light-sensitive for a few hours, so arrange not to drive and bring sunglasses.

### Should I see an eye doctor or an optometrist?

For glasses and basic vision testing, an optometrist is fine. For any eye disease, pain, sudden changes, diabetes-related screening, or surgery, see an eye doctor (ophthalmologist), who is medically qualified to diagnose and treat.
