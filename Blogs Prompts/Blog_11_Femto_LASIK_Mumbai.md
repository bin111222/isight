# Femto LASIK in Mumbai: How It Differs from Bladeless LASIK

If you have been researching LASIK surgery in Mumbai, you have almost certainly seen both "Femto LASIK" and "Bladeless LASIK" used as if they are different procedures — sometimes by different clinics marketing what is fundamentally the same surgery. This guide clears up the confusion immediately, explains what femtosecond technology actually does inside your eye, and gives you a complete picture of costs, candidacy, and outcomes for Femto LASIK in Mumbai in 2026.

---

## Clearing the Confusion: Femto LASIK = Bladeless LASIK

They are the same procedure.

**Femto LASIK** refers to LASIK in which the corneal flap is created using a **femtosecond laser** rather than a mechanical microkeratome (a small oscillating blade). Because no blade touches the eye, the same procedure is marketed as "Bladeless LASIK" or "All-Laser LASIK."

The terms are used interchangeably throughout Mumbai's refractive surgery centres. When a clinic says "Bladeless LASIK," they mean Femto LASIK. When another says "All-Laser LASIK," they also mean Femto LASIK. There is no meaningful clinical difference between these names.

What matters is which femtosecond laser platform the clinic uses for flap creation, and which excimer laser platform is used for the corneal reshaping step. Leading systems in Mumbai include the Alcon IntraLase, Ziemer FEMTO LDV, and Carl Zeiss VisuMax (also used for SMILE) for the flap, and the Alcon WaveLight EX500 or SCHWIND AMARIS for the ablation.

## What Is a Femtosecond Laser?

A femtosecond is one quadrillionth of a second (10⁻¹⁵ seconds). Femtosecond lasers fire pulses of infrared light so short that they interact with tissue through **photodisruption** rather than heat.

### How Photodisruption Works

When an ultra-short laser pulse is focused at a precise point in tissue, it creates a tiny bubble of plasma — vaporised water and carbon dioxide — in an area smaller than a red blood cell. Thousands of these microexplosions in a precise pattern create a clean tissue plane. The surrounding tissue is unaffected because the energy is so focused and the pulse so brief that there is essentially no collateral thermal damage.

In Femto LASIK, the laser creates thousands of these microbubbles in a programmed pattern inside your cornea, forming the corneal flap. The flap is then lifted and folded back so the excimer laser can reshape the corneal tissue underneath.

This is fundamentally different from a microkeratome, which oscillates a steel blade across the corneal surface to mechanically cut the flap — a process that is effective but less precise and carries higher variation in flap thickness.

## Femto LASIK vs Standard (Microkeratome) LASIK

| Feature | Standard LASIK (Microkeratome) | Femto (Bladeless) LASIK |
|---|---|---|
| Flap creation method | Mechanical oscillating blade | Femtosecond laser (photodisruption) |
| Flap thickness uniformity | Variable (±20–30 microns) | Highly uniform (±5 microns) |
| Flap complication rate | ~1 in 1,000 | ~1 in 10,000 |
| Customisable flap diameter | Limited | Yes — precise to 0.1mm |
| Suitability for thinner corneas | Lower precision | Better — laser allows thinner safe flaps |
| Risk of buttonhole or free cap | Low but present | Very rare |
| Patient comfort during flap step | Pressure sensation | Similar — brief suction |
| Cost per eye (Mumbai, 2026) | ₹20,000–₹40,000 | ₹55,000–₹85,000 |
| Post-flap excimer step | Same | Same |

The flap complication rate difference is significant. An irregular or incomplete flap created by a microkeratome can lead to postponed surgery, repeat procedures, or in rare cases, visual complications. With a femtosecond laser, the flap dimensions are programmed in advance — thickness, diameter, hinge angle — and executed with near-perfect reproducibility.

## Femto LASIK vs SMILE

Both procedures use femtosecond laser technology, but they use it in fundamentally different ways.

| Feature | Femto LASIK | SMILE |
|---|---|---|
| Flap created? | Yes | No — flapless |
| Excimer laser used? | Yes (reshapes cornea after flap) | No — lenticule extraction only |
| Treats hyperopia? | Yes | No |
| Treats myopia? | Yes | Yes (up to −10D) |
| Post-op dry eye risk | Moderate | Low |
| Corneal nerve preservation | Partial (nerves cut by flap) | High (nerves largely preserved) |
| Suitable for contact sports | With caution | Preferred |
| Topography-guided option available? | Yes (Contoura Vision) | Limited |
| Long-term data | 15+ years | 15+ years |
| Recovery to clear vision | 24 hours | 3–7 days |
| Cost per eye (Mumbai, 2026) | ₹55,000–₹85,000 | ₹80,000–₹1,05,000 |

The choice between Femto LASIK and SMILE typically comes down to: dry eye history, lifestyle (contact sports), prescription type (hyperopia is not treatable with SMILE), and whether you want topography-guided treatment (Contoura is available with Femto LASIK).

## The Femto LASIK Procedure in Mumbai: Step by Step

A complete Femto LASIK session at a Mumbai clinic takes approximately 30–45 minutes, though the actual laser time is far shorter.

### Step 1: Pre-operative Assessment (1–2 Hours, Often a Separate Visit)

Corneal topography, pachymetry, wavefront aberrometry, and dilated refraction are performed. These tests confirm your suitability, map your cornea, and define the precise treatment parameters.

### Step 2: Preparation in the Laser Suite

Anaesthetic eye drops are instilled. You lie comfortably under the laser system. A speculum gently holds your eyelids open. The laser interface is aligned to your eye.

### Step 3: Femtosecond Flap Creation (Approximately 20–30 Seconds Per Eye)

Gentle suction is applied. You may notice brief pressure and your vision dims momentarily — this is normal. The femtosecond laser fires thousands of precisely placed pulses to create the flap at the programmed depth (typically 90–110 microns). The suction is released.

### Step 4: Flap Lifting

Your surgeon gently separates the flap along the laser-defined plane using a fine instrument and folds it back, exposing the stromal bed beneath.

### Step 5: Excimer Laser Ablation (Approximately 20–40 Seconds Per Eye)

You fix your gaze on a target light. The excimer laser reshapes your cornea based on your prescription — and, if Contoura treatment is being delivered, based on your 22,000-point topography map. An active eye-tracking system (typically running at 1,050 Hz) compensates for any micro-movements.

### Step 6: Flap Repositioning

The flap is folded back into position and irrigated gently. It adheres naturally through surface tension — no stitches required. Your surgeon inspects the flap edges under the microscope.

### Step 7: Immediate Post-operative Care

Antibiotic and steroid eye drops are prescribed. You rest for a few hours. Most Mumbai patients report clear or near-clear vision by the following morning.

## Who Should Choose Femto LASIK?

Femto LASIK is the right choice for:

- **Patients with hyperopia** (farsightedness) — SMILE cannot treat hyperopia; Femto LASIK can
- **Patients who want topography-guided precision** — Contoura Vision is available with Femto LASIK; this is the best current option for treating higher-order aberrations with a flap-based approach
- **Patients with prescriptions that include both myopia and hyperopia components** (mixed astigmatism)
- **Patients who want the fastest visual recovery** — Femto LASIK typically delivers clear vision within 24 hours; SMILE takes 3–7 days
- **Patients who are SMILE candidates on paper but have significant higher-order aberrations** — Contoura LASIK may give better optical quality in these cases
- **Patients with corneas that are at the lower edge of adequate thickness** — the precise flap depth control of femtosecond flap creation leaves more residual stromal bed than a microkeratome in borderline cases

Femto LASIK (without Contoura) is a strong, proven procedure for most straightforward prescriptions. If you have a history of dry eye or play contact sports, SMILE deserves careful consideration instead.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Femto LASIK vs SMILE: which is better?

Neither is universally "better." Femto LASIK is preferable for hyperopia, fastest recovery, and topography-guided treatment (Contoura). SMILE is preferable for patients with dry eye tendencies, those in contact sports, and high myopes who want to preserve corneal biomechanics. Your surgeon should make this recommendation after reviewing your specific maps and lifestyle.

### Is Femto LASIK the same as Bladeless LASIK?

Yes — completely. Femto LASIK, Bladeless LASIK, and All-Laser LASIK are different marketing names for the same procedure: LASIK with a femtosecond laser used to create the flap instead of a microkeratome blade.

### What is the Femto LASIK cost in Mumbai?

Femto LASIK costs ₹55,000–₹85,000 per eye at reputable centres in Mumbai in 2026. Both eyes together typically cost ₹1,10,000–₹1,70,000 as a complete package.

### Is Femto LASIK safe?

Yes. Femto LASIK has one of the most extensive safety records in elective surgery. The flap complication rate with femtosecond laser is approximately 1 in 10,000 — ten times lower than with a microkeratome. The overall serious complication rate for experienced surgeons at well-equipped clinics is well below 0.5%.
