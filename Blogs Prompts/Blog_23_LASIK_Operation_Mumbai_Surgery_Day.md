On surgery day, a LASIK operation in Mumbai takes roughly 10–15 minutes of laser time per eye, with your total clinic stay running two to three hours including prep, the procedure, and post-op rest. The surgery itself is painless — you are fully awake, local anaesthetic drops numb the eye completely, and most patients are surprised by how fast and quiet the experience is.

This article walks you through every stage of the day: what to do the night before, how the laser room actually works, what you will feel in the first hour after surgery, and what to do if anything feels unusual once you get home.

## The Night Before Your LASIK Operation in Mumbai

Preparation the evening before surgery matters more than most patients expect. Small mistakes — sleeping in contact lenses, wearing perfume — can create complications on surgery day.

### Your night-before checklist

- **Remove contact lenses** — soft lenses must come out at least 24 hours before; rigid gas-permeable (RGP) lenses require 2–4 weeks off before the pre-op measurements, so confirm this with your surgeon's team
- **No alcohol** — dehydrates the cornea and interferes with healing
- **Wash hair** — you cannot get water in your eyes for a week, so this is your last easy wash
- **Lay out comfortable, front-opening clothing** — no pullovers that drag across your face
- **Arrange your escort** — Mumbai traffic is unpredictable; confirm pickup time the night before
- **Charge your phone and prepare sunglasses** — you will need dark glasses immediately after the procedure
- **Eat a normal dinner** — you are not going under general anaesthesia, so there is no strict fasting rule; a light meal two hours before the appointment is fine, but avoid a heavy meal immediately before

## Surgery Day Morning: What to Do Before Leaving Home

The morning protocol is short but non-negotiable.

**Do:**
- Have a light meal 2 hours before your appointment time
- Take any prescribed pre-op medications (some clinics prescribe a mild anti-anxiety tablet — confirm with your doctor)
- Wear loose, comfortable clothing
- Bring your prescription glasses as backup

**Do not:**
- Apply any eye makeup — mascara, kajal, eyeliner, or eye shadow
- Use perfume, deodorant spray, or hairspray (the aerosol particles can interfere with the excimer laser beam in the operating room)
- Wear contact lenses
- Drive yourself — arrange an escort, because you will not be able to drive home after the procedure

## Arriving at the Clinic: Registration and Pre-Op Prep (30–45 Minutes)

When you arrive at the clinic for your LASIK operation in Mumbai, expect to spend 30–45 minutes on pre-op preparation before you enter the laser room.

### What happens during pre-op prep

1. **Registration and consent forms** — you will review and sign informed consent documents. Read them carefully; ask questions if anything is unclear.
2. **Topical anaesthetic drops** — the nurse will instil numbing drops into both eyes. These take effect within minutes and completely eliminate discomfort during the procedure.
3. **Antibiotic drops** — a preventive dose is applied before the laser.
4. **Dilation drops (if required)** — some protocols use dilating drops for a final pre-op check; this varies by clinic and procedure type.
5. **Pre-op measurements** — the technician may re-confirm pupil size and corneal measurements, especially if this is your first visit of the day rather than a pre-booked slot.
6. **You wait** — sitting quietly in a dim room helps your eyes relax before surgery.

Wear your glasses in the waiting area. The staff will call you in when the laser room is ready.

## Inside the Laser Room: Step-by-Step

This is the part most patients are most anxious about. The reality is calmer than the anticipation.

### Step 1: Positioning under the laser

You lie flat on a padded table directly beneath the laser system. The room is cool and slightly dim. The surgeon and a technician are present throughout.

### Step 2: Eyelid holder (speculum)

A small plastic eyelid holder — called a speculum — is placed between your eyelids. This prevents blinking. You will feel gentle pressure, not pain. Your instinct to blink will disappear once the speculum is in place.

### Step 3: Flap creation (for standard LASIK and Femto LASIK)

For **bladeless/femtosecond LASIK**, a femtosecond laser creates the corneal flap. You will feel a brief pressure sensation — roughly 20 seconds per eye — as the suction ring holds the eye steady. Your vision may go dark or grey for a moment. This is normal and temporary.

For **SMILE LASIK**, there is no flap; the laser creates a lenticule inside the cornea and the surgeon removes it through a small incision. The pressure sensation is similar.

### Step 4: Excimer laser treatment

The surgeon lifts the flap (or completes the SMILE incision) and the excimer laser is applied. You will:
- Hear a rapid clicking sound
- Notice a faint smell — this is the laser vaporising microscopic amounts of corneal tissue and is entirely normal
- Be asked to look at a blinking fixation light; keep your gaze relaxed and steady
- Feel nothing — the numbing drops are fully effective

The laser runs for **10–40 seconds per eye** depending on your prescription. Higher prescriptions require more laser pulses; your surgeon will have told you the approximate duration during the consultation.

### Step 5: Flap repositioning

For LASIK (not SMILE), the surgeon gently repositions the flap. It adheres naturally within seconds. The surgeon checks the alignment, irrigates the surface, and fits a clear protective shield over the eye.

The entire process for both eyes takes **10–15 minutes** from the time you lie down.

## The First 30 Minutes After Surgery: What You Will Feel

Immediately after the LASIK operation in Mumbai, your vision will be foggy — as though you are looking through frosted glass or a steamy window. This is normal. The fogginess improves significantly within the first hour and continues to sharpen over the following 24–72 hours.

**Common sensations in the first 30 minutes:**
- Mild grittiness or a sensation that something is in the eye
- Watering or tearing
- Light sensitivity — even moderate indoor light can feel bright
- A burning sensation that fades within 20–30 minutes

**What you should do:**
- Keep your eyes closed as much as possible
- Do not rub your eyes under any circumstances — the flap is healing and rubbing can displace it
- The nurse will guide you to a dark, quiet recovery room

---

## Post-Op Rest and Discharge Process

You will rest in a dimly lit room for **30–60 minutes** with your eyes closed. This is the single most protective thing you can do in the first hour — it allows the initial healing to begin and gives the numbing drops time to wear off.

Before discharge, the surgeon or a trained technician will examine your eyes to confirm:
- The corneal flap is correctly positioned (for LASIK)
- There are no early complications
- Your vision has begun to clear

### Your discharge pack will include:
- **Antibiotic eye drops** — typically 4 times daily for one week
- **Steroid eye drops** — typically 4 times daily, tapering over 2–4 weeks
- **Lubricant eye drops** — use freely; they are preservative-free and comfortable
- **Plastic eye shields** — wear over both eyes every night for at least one week
- **Written post-op instructions** — including emergency contact details

You will be given a **next-day follow-up appointment**. Do not miss it.

## The Drive Home and the First Sleep

You cannot drive after a LASIK operation in Mumbai. Your escort must take you home. Keep your eyes closed or lightly shut during the journey and wear your sunglasses outdoors.

Once home:
1. **Sleep as soon as you can** — sleep is the most powerful healing tool in the first 24 hours. Even a 3–4 hour nap significantly accelerates recovery.
2. **Fit your eye shields before sleeping** — tape them gently over each eye so they cannot be rubbed or knocked during sleep.
3. **Avoid screens** for the first 4–6 hours post-surgery. If you must use a phone, keep brightness very low and use for no more than 10 minutes at a stretch.
4. **Do not shower with water running over your face** on the first night — wash your face with a damp cloth away from the eyes.

## Emergency Scenarios: What to Do If Something Feels Wrong

The vast majority of LASIK surgeries in Mumbai proceed without any post-operative complications. However, you should know what to watch for.

### Call the clinic immediately if you experience:
- Sudden sharp pain (mild grittiness is normal; sharp pain is not)
- Significant worsening of vision after the initial fogginess cleared
- Visible blood in the eye
- A sensation of the flap shifting (rare, but possible with eye rubbing)
- Intense, prolonged burning more than two hours after surgery

Every reputable clinic operating LASIK in Mumbai provides a 24-hour emergency contact number. Make sure you have it in your phone before you leave the clinic.

### What is normal and not an emergency:
- Mild watering for 12–24 hours — normal
- Halos around lights at night for the first 1–4 weeks — normal, resolves as the cornea stabilises
- Dry eye sensation requiring frequent lubricant drops — normal, common for 1–3 months
- Fluctuating vision between morning and evening for 2–4 weeks — normal

## FAQ

**What happens during LASIK surgery?**
During a LASIK operation in Mumbai, the surgeon first creates a thin corneal flap using either a blade microkeratome or a femtosecond laser. The flap is lifted and an excimer laser reshapes the underlying cornea to correct your refractive error. The flap is then repositioned. The entire procedure takes 10–15 minutes for both eyes and you are awake throughout.

**Is LASIK operation painful?**
No. Topical anaesthetic drops numb the eye completely before the procedure begins. You may feel mild pressure during flap creation (about 20 seconds) but this is pressure, not pain. Most patients describe the experience as surprisingly comfortable.

**How long does LASIK take?**
The laser room time is 10–15 minutes for both eyes. The excimer laser itself runs for 10–40 seconds per eye depending on your prescription. Add 30–45 minutes for pre-op preparation and 30–60 minutes for post-op rest, and your total clinic stay is typically two to three hours.

**What to expect on LASIK surgery day?**
Expect to arrive at the clinic and spend 30–45 minutes on pre-op preparation including numbing drops and final checks. The procedure takes 10–15 minutes. You rest for 30–60 minutes, receive a discharge check, and go home with a prescription bag. Your vision will be foggy initially and will sharpen significantly within 24–48 hours. The next-day follow-up is mandatory.

**Can I eat before LASIK surgery?**
Yes. A light meal two hours before your appointment is fine. Avoid a heavy meal immediately before the procedure. There is no general anaesthesia involved, so strict fasting is not required.
